Seasonal variability of climate and micrometeorological CO2 fluxes across amazonian and Cerrado tower sites

Humberto da Rocha, USP, humberto@model.iag.usp.br (Presenting)
Michael Goulden, UCI, mgoulden@uci.edu
Scott Miller, UCI, SUNY, sdmiller@uci.edu
Antonio Manzi, INPA, manzi@inpa.gov.br
Antonio Nobre, INPA,INPE, anobre@ltid.inpe.br
Steven C. Wofsy, Harvard, scw@io.harvard.edu
Scott R. Saleska, Harvard, UoA, saleska@fas.harvard.edu
Osvaldo Cabral, Embrapa, ocabral@cnpma.embrapa.br
Bart Kruijt, Alterra, Bart.Kruijt@wur.nl

Climate data and CO2 atmospheric fluxes from six micrometeorological flux towers across the Amazonian and Cerrado biomes are compared on the seasonal variability. 15 day mean gross primary productivity at the sites is estimated using the daytime 30 min atmospheric CO2 fluxes. There appear to be a remarkable pattern of min/max GPP year round each site, concurrent with the length and intensity of the dry season each site.
